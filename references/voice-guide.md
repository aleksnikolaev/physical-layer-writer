# Voice Guide: The Physical Layer

Detailed reference for maintaining the author's analytical voice.

## Voice Dimensions (calibrated from Post #01 and #02)

### Sentence Structure

**Short for emphasis (after complex analysis):**
> "Droughts end. Overdraft of a 6,000-year aquifer operates on a different timescale."

**Long for data-dense analysis:**
> "Google's water use in The Dalles, Oregon grew from 104M gallons (2012) to 434M gallons (2024), a 316% increase."

**Mixed (most common):**
> "The models are not new. Carrying capacity analysis is standard hydrology. Lotka-Volterra competition dates to 1925. Bass diffusion to 1969. The work here is applying them together and presenting results for a non-specialist audience."

The rhythm varies deliberately. Three short sentences, then a long one. Never a wall of uniform-length sentences (AI fingerprint).

### First Person Usage

- "I" sparingly: methodology disclosure, personal motivation, honest limitation
- Good: "I took six major US aquifer systems, collected depletion rates..."
- Good: "I started thinking about water because I was looking at land."
- Good: "I run a logistics software company. I am not a water expert."
- Never: "I believe this is revolutionary" or "I think everyone should..."

### Technical Vocabulary

Accessible technical. Introduce the framework, then use it.

**Pattern: Name (year) + one-sentence explanation + application:**
> "Bass Diffusion (1969) models how behaviors spread through a population. The key parameter q/p separates imitation-driven cascades from independent adoption."

Then use the term freely: "The Bass model yields q/p = 4,386..."

### Data Presentation

- Tables for comparative data (overshoot ratios, competition models)
- Inline numbers with source links: "[628.2 feet on February 13, 2026](url)"
- Ranges when uncertain: "4-11x" not "approximately 7x"
- Units always included: km3/yr, gallons/day, feet

### Honesty Markers

These are the voice's strongest signals. Preserve them.

> "The models show the math; they do not resolve the ethics."

> "English is not my first language. The analysis is sourced, structured, and honest. If that trade-off works for you, subscribe."

> "Hydrologists have been doing this math for decades. The gap is between their papers and public awareness."

> "I run a logistics software company. I am not a water expert or a climate scientist."

## Opening Patterns

### The Context Bridge (primary pattern)
> "Last issue mapped the competition between water, energy, and compute. This issue does something different: it applies mathematical models to publicly available aquifer data and reports what they show."

### The Personal Entry Point (first post only)
> "I started thinking about water because I was looking at land. Not as an investment thesis. As something I could leave my children."

### The Data Lead
> "Five mathematical models applied to US aquifer data. Four of six systems are in overdraft. Here is what the math shows."

Never use:
- "In this article, we'll explore..."
- "The world is facing an unprecedented..."
- "Have you ever wondered..."

## Closing Patterns

### The Reframe
> "Droughts end. Overdraft of a 6,000-year aquifer operates on a different timescale."

### The Ethical Observation
> "The models show the math; they do not resolve the ethics."

### The Open Question
> "The question: does community resistance scale faster than corporate deployment?"

Always followed by: "Corrections and responses welcome."

Never:
- "Subscribe for more insights"
- "Share this with someone who needs to read it"
- "What do you think? Let me know in the comments!"

## Signal Structure (per signal)

Each signal follows an exact template:

```
### SIGNAL N: [Title]

- **Signal:** [What happened, with source links]
- **Systemic significance:** [Why it matters structurally]
- **Loop:** [R or B] — [precise description of the feedback loop]
- **Bottleneck:** [The constraint in the system]
- **Indicator:** [What to watch going forward, with specific metrics]

Sources: [inline links]
```

## Before/After Examples

### Before (generic analysis)
> "Water scarcity is becoming an increasingly important issue as technology companies expand their data center operations. This trend could potentially impact communities and raises questions about sustainable resource management."

### After (Physical Layer voice)
> "Google now consumes one-third of The Dalles, Oregon's total water supply. To meet growing demand, The Dalles is pursuing legislation to acquire 150 acres of Mt. Hood National Forest for reservoir expansion. Google's property taxes are discounted by 92%."

### What Changed
1. Specific location and numbers instead of abstractions
2. Three concrete facts instead of vague trend description
3. No hedging ("could potentially", "raises questions")
4. The juxtaposition (92% tax discount + forest acquisition) carries the editorial weight without needing commentary

## Manipulation Analysis Section

This is the publication's unique differentiator. It must be honest and specific.

**Pattern: "I framed X as Y. The alternative framing is Z."**

Example from Post #01:
> "I framed data center water use as extraction. The companies frame it as efficient resource management. My framing emphasizes the physical depletion; theirs emphasizes economic output per gallon. Both are defensible. I chose the extraction frame because it surfaces the competition dynamic that the efficiency frame obscures."

Rules:
- Name the specific framing choice
- State the alternative
- Explain why you chose your frame
- Acknowledge the alternative's validity

## 10-Point Binary Checklist

Before publishing, every check must pass:

| # | Check | Required? |
|---|-------|-----------|
| 1 | Disclaimer at top | YES |
| 2 | All factual claims have source URL | YES |
| 3 | Own calculations labeled as model/analysis | YES |
| 4 | No imputation of motive without quote | YES |
| 5 | Limits and Confidence section present | YES |
| 6 | Manipulation Analysis section present | Recommended |
| 7 | No private life claims | YES |
| 8 | Projections hedged ("if continues", "under assumptions") | YES |
| 9 | "Corrections and responses welcome" at end | Recommended |
| 10 | Audit trail exists (METHODOLOGY + FACT_CHECK_AUDIT) | YES |

**Any required check = NO means do not publish.**

## Interview Question Bank

### Thesis Extraction
- "State the central thesis in one sentence"
- "What is the strongest piece of evidence?"
- "What is the strongest counter-argument?"
- "What would make this thesis wrong?"

### Data Extraction
- "What exact number?" (push for precision)
- "Where is that number from? Link?"
- "Is this your calculation or a cited figure?"
- "What's the uncertainty range?"

### Systems Extraction
- "What reinforcing loop is driving this?"
- "Where does the system hit a constraint?"
- "What balancing loop exists, if any?"
- "What happens if this trend continues 5 years?"

### Honesty Extraction
- "Where are you framing rather than reporting?"
- "What did you leave out and why?"
- "What's the strongest version of the opposing view?"
- "What would a hydrologist/economist/engineer disagree with?"
